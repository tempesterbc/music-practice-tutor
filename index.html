Sheet music

Only have a photo or screenshot of the music?

Scanning printed music from a photo (optical music recognition) needs a dedicated engine to be accurate. Convert your photo to a MusicXML file with one of these free tools, then upload it above — it'll read exactly and unlock the engraved score, play-along, and analysis.

  1. PlayScore 2 (phone app) — photograph the page, then export MusicXML. playscore.co
  2. MuseScore 4 (free desktop) — import your PDF/score, check it, then File → Export → MusicXML. musescore.org

Single-line parts scan most reliably — always glance over the result before trusting the analysis. Chords and dense piano scores are hardest.

    How it works

    1

    Build the corridor

    Several professional recordings are aligned in time (Dynamic Time Warping) and fused into a “professional corridor” — the mean and the acceptable range for pitch, loudness, brightness and breathiness at every moment.

    2

    Compare you to it

    Your recording is aligned onto the same timeline. Wherever you leave the corridor for a sustained stretch, that spot is flagged and mapped back to a time in your own take.

    3

    Prescribe exercises

    Each flagged problem is matched to targeted exercises from a curated database of 70+ studies — instrument-aware, with the reasoning and steps.

    What it detects

    Precise per-note rhythm (rushing/dragging) is handled by a companion score-based engine; the corridor engine focuses on tone, intonation, dynamics and timbre.
