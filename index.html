    How it works

    1

    Build the corridor

    Several professional recordings are aligned in time (Dynamic Time Warping) and fused into a “professional corridor” — the mean and the acceptable range for pitch, loudness, brightness and breathiness at every moment.

    2

    Compare you to it

    Your recording is aligned onto the same timeline. Wherever you leave the corridor for a sustained stretch, that spot is flagged and mapped back to a time in your own take.

    3

    Prescribe exercises

    Each flagged problem is matched to targeted exercises from a curated database of 70+ studies — instrument-aware, with the reasoning and steps.

    What it detects

    Precise per-note rhythm (rushing/dragging) is handled by a companion score-based engine; the corridor engine focuses on tone, intonation, dynamics and timbre.
